How to Change Engine Oil

Learn how to drain old oil and replace the filter to keep your engine running smoothly.

  1. Prepare the vehicle — Park on a level surface, apply parking brake, and let engine cool for 10 minutes.
  2. Lift the front — Use a floor jack to raise the front of the vehicle and secure with jack stands.
  3. Drain the oil — Locate the drain plug, place an oil catch pan underneath, and remove the plug.
  4. Replace the filter — Use an oil filter wrench to remove the old filter, apply oil to the new filter gasket, and install.
  5. Add new oil — Replace the drain plug, lower the vehicle, and add the recommended oil type and quantity.

Medium 45 mins

How to Replace Brake Pads

Replace worn brake pads to maintain stopping power and prevent damage to rotors.

  1. Loosen lug nuts — While wheel is on ground, loosen the lug nuts on the wheel you'll be working on.
  2. Lift and secure — Lift the vehicle with a jack and place jack stands at the proper jack points.
  3. Remove caliper — Remove caliper bolts and hang caliper with wire to avoid straining the brake line.
  4. Remove old pads — Take out worn brake pads and note which direction the wear indicators face.
  5. Install new pads — Apply brake grease to contact points, install new pads, reassemble caliper, and torque bolts.

Easy 10 mins

How to Jump-Start a Dead Battery

Get your car running again with these simple jump-starting steps using jumper cables.

  1. Position vehicles — Park the donor vehicle facing the dead car, close enough for cables to reach.
  2. Connect positive — Attach red clamp to positive (+) terminal on dead battery.
  3. Connect negative — Attach red clamp to positive on donor battery, black clamp to negative on donor.
  4. Ground connection — Connect black clamp to unpainted metal on the dead car's engine block.
  5. Start and remove — Start donor car, wait 2 minutes, start dead car, then remove cables in reverse order.

Easy 10 mins

How to Replace an Air Filter

Improve engine performance and fuel efficiency with a clean air filter.

  1. Locate the filter housing — Find the air filter box under the hood, usually near the engine intake.
  2. Open the housing — Unclip or unscrew the clips holding the filter cover in place.
  3. Remove old filter — Take out the old air filter and note the direction of airflow indicated on it.
  4. Check and clean — Wipe out any debris from the housing with a clean cloth.
  5. Install new filter — Place new filter with correct airflow direction, secure cover and clips.

Easy 10 mins

How to Check and Top Up Coolant

Keep your engine at the right temperature by checking coolant levels regularly.

  1. Cool down — Wait for the engine to cool completely (at least 30 minutes) before opening the reservoir.
  2. Locate reservoir — Find the translucent coolant reservoir usually marked with "MAX" and "MIN" lines.
  3. Check level — The coolant should be between the MIN and MAX lines when the engine is cold.
  4. Add coolant — If low, slowly add the recommended coolant type until reaching the MAX line.
  5. Check for leaks — Run engine and watch for any coolant leaks or steam from the system.

Easy 10 mins

How to Replace Windshield Wipers

Ensure clear visibility in all weather conditions with fresh wiper blades.

  1. Lift the wiper arm — Lift the wiper arm away from the windshield until it stays in raised position.
  2. Find release mechanism — Locate the small tab or button that releases the blade from the arm.
  3. Remove old blade — Press the release tab and slide the old wiper blade off the arm.
  4. Attach new blade — Slide the new blade onto the arm until it clicks into place.
  5. Test — Lower the arm and test the wipers on low and high speeds for smooth operation.

Medium 30 mins

How to Fix a Flat Tire

Learn to safely change a flat tire and get back on the road quickly.

  1. Pull over safely — Move to a flat, stable area away from traffic and engage hazard lights.
  2. Loosen lug nuts — While wheel is on ground, break lug nuts loose by turning counterclockwise.
  3. Lift the vehicle — Place jack at correct jack point and lift until tire is off the ground.
  4. Remove wheel — Remove lug nuts completely, take off flat tire, and place spare on hub.
  5. Secure wheel — Hand-tighten lug nuts, lower vehicle, and fully torque in a star pattern.
⚡
Medium 60 mins

How to Replace Spark Plugs

Restore engine power and fuel efficiency with new spark plugs.

  1. Access engine — Open hood, locate spark plug wires or ignition coil packs on engine.
  2. Remove wires/coils — Carefully disconnect ignition coils or spark plug wires, noting their positions.
  3. Remove old plugs — Use spark plug socket to remove old plugs, checking gap if required.
  4. Gap new plugs — Set new spark plug gap according to manufacturer specifications using a gap tool.
  5. Install and reassemble — Hand-start new plugs, torque to spec, reconnect coils/wires in correct order.
🔧
Hard 90 mins

How to Clean a Throttle Body

Improve idle quality and throttle response by cleaning carbon buildup.

  1. Disconnect battery — Remove negative battery terminal to prevent electrical issues.
  2. Locate throttle body — Find the throttle body between air intake and intake manifold.
  3. Remove air intake — Loosen clamps and remove air intake hose connected to throttle body.
  4. Clean throttle plate — Spray throttle body cleaner on a brush and clean around the throttle plate.
  5. Reassemble — Reinstall intake hose, reconnect battery, start engine and let it idle for 10 minutes.
